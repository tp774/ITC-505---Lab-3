How to Serve Custom Fonts

For this lab, I went with the font "Creepster", and trust me, this one’s a bit different from your usual choices. Imagine walking into a haunted house—the sign above the door is written in letters that look like they’re dripping down, almost as if they’re melting right in front of you. That’s Creepster for you. It’s spooky, fun, and full of character!

Now, would you use this font for a business report? Probably not! But for something like a Halloween party flyer or a mystery novel cover? Absolutely! Creepster is like the rebellious cousin of the more serious fonts we all know. It’s bold, playful, and definitely attention-grabbing. And the best part? Despite all its spooky vibes, it’s still totally readable—no weird symbols or hard-to-decipher letters here.

So, whether you're putting together a quirky blog post, an event invitation, or just want to spice up your webpage, Creepster has got your back. It’s the font equivalent of putting on a costume and having some fun with your text. It’s bold, a little bit eerie, but doesn’t take itself too seriously. And that’s what makes it perfect for standing out in a sea of boring fonts.
